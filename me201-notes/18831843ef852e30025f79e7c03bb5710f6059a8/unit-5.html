Unit 5

Unit 2 covers three main topics: Cartesian Vectors, Vector angles (alpha, beta, gamma), and angles between two vectors.

Cartesian vector notation is a notation that shows how much of a vector is in all 3 dimensions. Normal vectors typically are shown in magnitude form, which doesnâ€™t tell us where the vector goes. We also learn how to do addition with cartesian vectors. All of this is taught in HW 2.1.

Vector angles help tell us where a vector is pointed without a coordinate grid. Alpha is associated with the angle of the vector in relation to the x-axis. Beta is associated with the angle of the vector in relation to the y-axis. Finally, Gamma is associated with the angle of the vector in relation to the z-axis. This is covered in HW 2.2.

Angles between two vectors are typically one of the lesser-used concepts within engineering, but nevertheless, it is taught in this class. We learn to calculate the direct angle between two vectors or even between a vector and a line. We ignore alpha, beta, and gamma as we are getting the 3-D angle, rather than 2-D angles. This concept is covered in HW 2.3.
